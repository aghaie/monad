# Irreducible Principles Report — Phase 8 (F)

**Status:** complete. **Date:** 2026-06-06. **Method version:**
`phase8-principles-1.0`.

Phase F searches for principles that cannot be reduced further: clusters of
principles that mutually depend on one another (cannot be ordered), and the
explanatory residue that no single principle generates. Purely graph-theoretic; no
meaning assigned.

---

## 1. Irreducible principle cluster

The principle dependency graph (`DEPENDS_ON ∪ REQUIRES` lifted to principles) has
**one strongly-connected component of size ≥ 2** — and it is large:

| Size | Internal dependency edges | Principles |
|---:|---:|---|
| **11** | many | `PRINCIPLE_002, 005, 007, 008, 009, 010, 011, 012, 013, 014, 015` |

**11 of the 16 principles form a single mutually-dependent cycle.** They cannot be
linearised, ranked, or reduced: removing any one breaks a dependency relation that
the others route around through the cycle. This is the principle-level counterpart
of Phase 5's 94-concept directional SCC — irreducibility reappears one level up.

The remaining 5 principles (`001, 003, 004, 006, 016`) sit outside the cycle as
sources, short branches, or the isolated singleton — but they do not constitute an
independent foundation either (they leak ≥ 90% of their relations into the cycle).

---

## 2. Irreducible explanatory residue

Beyond the dependency cycle, there is a second, stronger irreducibility:

> **6,153 of 6,832 relations (90.1%) are inter-principle** — generated by no
> single principle.

This residue is *irreducible to principles* by construction: each of these
relations connects concepts in different modules, so no module contains it. Adding
principles to a reconstruction set never captures them in the generating sense.
The 90.1% residue is the formal reason the internal coverage ceiling sits at 9.9%.

| Irreducibility | Magnitude |
|---|---|
| Largest irreducible principle cluster (dependency SCC) | **11 / 16 principles** |
| Irreducible explanatory residue (inter-principle relations) | **90.1%** of structure |

---

## 3. Why the cores from Phase 5 did not become principles

The Phase-5 size-9 irreducible **concept** SCC is split across five principles
(`002, 005, 010, 011, 015`). The modularity modules (semantic + proposition
co-membership) and the dependency SCCs (mutual `DEPENDS_ON`/`REQUIRES`) are
**orthogonal cuts** of the same graph. The dependency cycle therefore re-emerges
*between* principles rather than inside any one — which is exactly what produces
the single size-11 irreducible principle cluster.

---

## 4. Findings (no interpretation)

1. **The principle layer is globally cyclic.** A single size-11 SCC dominates;
   there is no reducible spine of independent principles.
2. **Most structure is principle-irreducible.** 90.1% of relations cross module
   boundaries and are generated by no principle.
3. **Irreducibility is intrinsic, not hub-induced.** It survives the same way the
   Phase-5 cores did — distributed across the periphery, not concentrated in one
   module.
4. **No irreducible *foundation* exists** — irreducibility here means
   *non-reducibility to principles*, not the existence of a small principled core.

## 6. Limitations

- SCCs are computed over Phase-4 `DEPENDS_ON`/`REQUIRES` with their fixed
  thresholds; a different relation population would redraw the cluster.
- "Irreducible" is a graph property (mutual reachability / cross-boundary
  membership), not a semantic claim.
