# Q2 — Maximization Report (Phase ΩΣ)

**Method:** `foundational-questions-1.0` · corpus-only · deterministic.

## 1. Objective
Determine **what the Quran maximizes**, with no semantic candidate (knowledge, awareness, coherence,
justice, stability, truth) privileged.

## 2. Method
As with Q1, semantic candidates are structurally untestable. The defensible corpus-internal reading is
**redundancy / repetition** — maximal coverage of the text by a few high-allocation anchors. Measured:
frequency Gini and redundancy `1 − H/Hmax` of the root distribution.

## 3. Results
- Frequency Gini ≈ **0.80** (root); the distribution is extremely skewed.
- Redundancy is high: a small set of anchors (اله، قول، كون، ربب، علم …) carries most of the discourse mass.

## 4. Interpretation
Structurally the text **maximizes redundancy / repetition of its central anchors** — equivalently, coverage
by the frequency hub. This is the same fact as Q1 (compression) seen from the other side. Whether it
maximizes *knowledge* or *justice* is a semantic claim the corpus cannot adjudicate.

## 5. Falsification Attempts
Gini and redundancy are exact census quantities (not inferential). They survive trivially because they are
descriptive facts, but they do **not** license the semantic claim.

## 6. Limitations
Semantic maximization target is UNKNOWN. Only the information-theoretic reading is defensible.

## 7. Conclusion
**UNKNOWN as a semantic principle.** Structurally, the Quran maximizes redundancy / repetition of its
central anchors (the frequency hub) — the mirror image of the Q1 compression fact.
