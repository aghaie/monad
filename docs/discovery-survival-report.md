## 4. Interpretation
Only **30%** of major discoveries survive a representation change — and they are all
**information-theoretic** (frequency, residual, non-predictivity, scale-invariance, coherence,
lexical-freedom), not conceptual. **55%** are representation-dependent or artifacts: the entire conceptual
edifice (concepts, propositions, compression, identities, grammar, semantics, world-model, methodology)
plus the failed/collapsed phases (principles, decision architecture, numerical structure). The conceptual
layer does not survive the removal of its representation.

## 5. Falsification Attempts
Each TYPE assignment is justified by a measured control (rebuild here, or ARI/null/prediction from a prior
phase); nothing is classified invariant without evidence.

## 6. Limitations
The classification is at the discovery level; partial within-discovery survival (e.g. identity anchors
moderate but counts method-relative) is noted in the evidence field.

## 7. Conclusion
**6/20 discoveries (30%) survive representation change — all information-theoretic; 11/20 (55%) are
representation-dependent or artifacts** — the conceptual edifice does not survive.
