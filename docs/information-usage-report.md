# Information-Usage Report — Phase Δ (C)

**Phase:** Δ · **Method version:** `decision-architecture-1.0` · **Date:** 2026-06-08.

## 1. Objective
Determine what information is present before decisions: what influences decisions, and what is ignored?

## 2. Method
Flow into and out of the knowledge node (علم), the system's information carrier.

## 3. Results
- **Into knowledge**: choice → knowledge (support 626), condition → knowledge (dir 0.60, 618),
  action → knowledge (316), conflict → knowledge (146).
- **Out of knowledge**: knowledge → resolution (dir 0.56, 183), knowledge → consequence (122),
  knowledge → uncertainty (dir 0.69, 114).

## 4. Interpretation
Knowledge (علم) is the system's most-connected node — almost every decision structure co-occurs with it.
The robust outflows are **knowledge → resolution** (knowing precedes judging) and **knowledge →
uncertainty** — but the latter is dominated by the *عالِم الغيب* ("knower of the unseen") collocation, a
fixed phrase, not an information-processing step. So information *usage* is mostly the ubiquity of علم
(a high-frequency hub), and the one decision-meaningful survivor is knowledge → resolution.

## 5. Falsification Attempts
Knowledge's ubiquity is largely frequency (it is among the highest-frequency roots, Phase 16); only
knowledge → resolution and the (collocational) knowledge → uncertainty survive controls.

## 6. Limitations
"Information" is operationalized as the knowledge node; distant-vs-local information is captured by
cross-ayah adjacency only.

## 7. Conclusion
**Information (knowledge/علم) is ubiquitous but mostly frequency-driven**; the one robust
information→decision step is **knowledge → resolution** (knowing before judging).
