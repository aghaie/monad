# Q14 — The Quran's Own Central Question Report (Phase ΩΣ)

**Method:** `foundational-questions-1.0` · corpus-only · deterministic.

## 1. Objective
Which question does the Quran **itself** spend the greatest effort answering — measured by discourse
allocation, repetition, and structural centrality, not by what humans or scholars ask?

## 2. Method
Two structural readings: (a) roots most **over-represented in interrogative (INTG) ayahs** (lift vs
document-frequency baseline) — what the text literally frames as questions; (b) **maximal discourse
allocation** — the roots with the highest document-frequency and co-occurrence degree. Arabic anchors are
evidence, never glossed.

## 3. Results
- **Maximal-allocation centre** (df): اله، قول، كون، ربب، علم، امن، قوم، اتي، كفر، بين.
- **Interrogative-associated roots** (lift, evidence): كيف (14.8×), اني (11.9×), دلل (11.5×), دري (8.8×),
  سير (7.4×), نظر (7.1×) — the text's questions cluster around *how/whence/show/know/look*.
- Interrogative ayahs: a minority of the corpus; the central-allocation hub is the same frequency hub as
  Q13.

## 4. Interpretation
The "central question" is **not recoverable as semantics**. Structurally, maximal effort = maximal discourse
allocation, which is the frequency hub (Q13/Q4). The interrogative structure points at the *same* high-
allocation anchors, with question-particles concentrating on *how/whence/observe/know* roots — reported as
evidence, not interpreted into a thesis. Any claim about *which* question (e.g. "the nature of God",
"accountability") would import meaning the corpus cannot supply.

## 5. Falsification Attempts
The interrogative lift is relative to the frequency baseline; the central-anchor claim is the frequency hub
itself and is therefore **not a beyond-frequency finding** — it reduces to frequency dominance.

## 6. Limitations
Discourse allocation is a frequency proxy; the semantic content of the "central question" is forbidden and
unknown.

## 7. Conclusion
**UNKNOWN as semantics.** Structurally, the Quran spends the most effort on its central frequency anchors;
its interrogatives concentrate on *how/whence/know/observe* roots (evidence only). The semantic "central
question" is not recoverable from the corpus.
