# Phase ΩΣ — Executive Summary

**Foundational Question Discovery Engine.** We asked the Quran the fourteen biggest questions about itself
and let the text — and only the text — answer. No theology, no interpretation, no outside data. Where the
corpus cannot decide, the answer is **UNKNOWN**, and that is treated as a real result.

## What we found

**Seven questions got real, measurable answers:**

- **Human or world model?** *Both.* A 3rd-person world (60%) spoken to a 2nd-person human listener (40%) —
  the text is pervasively **addressed to someone**.
- **Book or engine?** *Engine.* It is mostly commands, conditions, and ongoing action (53%), not a static
  catalogue of facts.
- **Objects or relations?** *Relations.* Meaning lives in how words connect (45 links per word), not in the
  words alone.
- **How is time modelled?** *As pattern, not timeline.* Past-as-example and present-address are balanced,
  with heavy repetition — recurrence, not chronology.
- **How much survives if you delete every name?** *~95%.* Names are only 5% of the text; remove them and the
  structure is unchanged. The Quran's structure does **not** depend on proper names.
- **Is there a hidden geometry (ring/symmetry)?** *No* — symmetric verse pairs are no more similar than
  random ordering. The only "self-similarity" is the ordinary statistical kind.
- **What is its essential core / what does it spend most effort on?** The handful of most-frequent anchor
  roots — i.e., the **frequency hub**, not a hidden meaning.

**Seven questions came back UNKNOWN or collapsed onto the frequency hub:**

- *What does it minimize / maximize?* Only an information-theoretic answer is honest (it compresses frequent
  forms and repeats its anchors); the semantic version is unknowable structurally.
- *Observable vs hidden reality?* Needs world knowledge — UNKNOWN.
- *What is left unsaid?* Silence is real (lots of pronouns), but its content is unknowable.
- *Revelation order vs canonical order?* The chronological order is **outside data and forbidden**, so this
  is UNKNOWN; the only intrinsic split (Meccan/Medinan) looks structurally the same.
- *The single "central question"?* As meaning, UNKNOWN; structurally it is just the most-repeated anchors.

## The verdict
**Partial success — exactly the honest outcome the design allowed.** The Quran, measured purely as
structure, is a **frequency-dominated, human-addressed, command-register, pattern-timed, name-independent,
relation-dense** text. The deepest-sounding questions about it all converge on one thing — its statistical
core — and the genuinely semantic questions, the project refuses to fake. Every answer that survived a
fairness test did so at root, lemma, and word level alike.
