# Phase 12 — Final Report: Generative Grammar Discovery Engine

**Status:** complete. **Date:** 2026-06-06. **Method version:**
`phase12-grammar-1.0`.

Phase 12 built the **Generative Grammar Discovery Engine** to test — never assume
— whether the observed Quranic proposition network can be *generated* by a small
set of structural production rules. A rule is an empirically-measured
transformation that, from an empty graph, repeatedly produces observed structure
— not a concept, not a motif. Rules carry opaque ids `RULE_001…`; none is named.
No meaning, theology, translation, or tafsir is used; no external graph theory is
cited as an explanation; **generation is claimed only where simulation confirms
it.** Phases 1–11 are read and hashed but never rebuilt. Deterministic,
pure-stdlib, byte-identically reproducible (`validate_grammar.py --rebuild`, **70
checks pass**).

---

## 1. Method

Three production rules were discovered, their parameters **measured** from the
observed graph (N = 100, M = 1,059): degree-proportional attachment (`RULE_001`,
γ = 1.0), reciprocity (`RULE_002`, fraction 0.38), transitive closure (`RULE_003`,
fraction 0.15). Mixing fractions were fit by deterministic grid search to match
the observed reciprocity (0.612) and transitivity (0.250); the triad distribution
was a **held-out** target. The model was then run from an empty graph (30 runs)
and compared to the observed network on triad distribution, reciprocity,
transitivity, hub share, and largest SCC, with 95% CIs.

---

## 2. Primary research question

> *Can the observed Quranic network be generated by a relatively small set of
> structural production rules?*

**Answer: partially — locally yes, globally no.** Three (effectively two) local
rules regenerate the **13-class motif vocabulary** at triad-distribution cosine
**0.905** from an empty graph, plus the reciprocity, transitivity, and giant-SCC
size. But they do **not** generate the extreme hub (only 39% of its magnitude) or
the Phase-10 consistency (out of topological scope). Generation is **partial and
local**, confirmed by simulation.

## 4. Rule count, robustness, accuracy

- **Rules:** 3 discovered; **2 effective** (RULE_001 attachment, RULE_002
  reciprocity). RULE_003 (transitive closure) contributes 0.005 cosine — near-
  redundant.
- **Generation accuracy (simulation, 30 runs):** triad cosine **0.905** [0.887,
  0.922]; motif classes 13/13; reciprocity ratio 0.97; transitivity ratio 1.04;
  SCC ratio 0.97; **hub ratio 0.39**.
- **Robustness:** rule parameters stable under 10–20% edge-removal (hub share
  0.088 [0.085, 0.091]; reciprocity/transitivity fall modestly but stay banded).

## 5. Ablation rankings

| Rank | Rule | Transformation | Cosine drop |
|---:|---|---|---:|
| 1 | `RULE_002` | reciprocity | **0.357 (essential)** |
| 2 | `RULE_001` | attachment | 0.027 |
| 3 | `RULE_003` | transitive closure | 0.005 (near-redundant) |

**The local structure is dominated by a single reciprocity process** on top of a
degree-biased edge budget. The recurring motifs are largely the shadow of one
rule.

## 6. Falsification results

1 generation claim **SURVIVES** (motif vocabulary), 2 **FALSIFIED** (hub
dominance; consistency — out of scope), 1 **PARTIAL** (SCC size yes, membership
untested). Increasing the attachment exponent γ never reproduces the hub — it only
degrades the motif match — confirming the hub is an **irreducible primitive**
(Phase 11: hub SURVIVES STRONGLY).

## 9. Limitations

- The grammar models **topology only**; the activation matrix and its consistency
  are out of scope (and thus genuinely not generable here).
- Generation accuracy is measured against the inherited Phase-4 proposition graph
  with its fixed thresholds.
- The simulator is one deterministic family of production rules; other rule
  families might reach similar cosine — the result shows *a* sufficient local
  grammar exists, not that it is unique.
- "Generation" is topological reproduction confirmed by simulation — no mechanism,
  intention, or meaning is claimed.
